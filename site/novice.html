Adjusted competitive pace

Was the front of each Novice A or B field faster or slower than other years?

The headline measure is the median of the top three boats—the second-place time—adjusted by how fast or slow a broad basket of other events ran that same day. An index above 100 is faster than the typical season in the same event and distance era. Men Novice B’s 2023 half-mile results are kept separate from the quarter-mile years.

1. Start at 100

100 is the typical adjusted pace for that event and distance. A dot to the right is faster; a dot to the left is slower.

2. Read the percentage

An index of 104 means the front of the field was about 4% faster than its comparison baseline after the race-day adjustment.

3. Check the context

Raw time shows what the clock recorded. The first-to-third gap shows front-field depth, and race count shows how much evidence supports the season.

90 · slower100 · typical110 · faster

This is a comparative indicator, not a claim about absolute boat speed. It reduces the influence of slow final boats and unusual race-day conditions; races need three valid finishers and at least eight benchmark events.

Here is how the chart produces a 2025 pace index of 101.3 and a 2026 pace index of 97.2.

1. Represent each race with the second-place time

2. Estimate whether each regatta ran fast or slow

For approximately 37–40 non-novice benchmark events at each regatta, the calculation compares that event’s top-three median with its median elsewhere in the same season. The median of those ratios becomes the race-day factor. A factor of 1.00 is typical, above 1.00 means the day ran slower, and below 1.00 means it ran faster.

For example, Waimanalo 2025 had a factor of 1.0661, meaning events ran about 6.6% slower than their season norms. Father’s Day 2026 had a factor of 0.9579, meaning events ran about 4.2% faster.

Waimanalo 2025 looked extremely slow at 4:17.07, but other events were also slow, so its adjusted time becomes 4:01.14. Father’s Day 2026 looked fast at 3:46.92, but the whole regatta ran fast, so its adjusted time becomes 3:56.90.

4. Calculate each season’s competitive pace

The median adjusted time is 230.58 seconds (3:50.58) for 2025 and 240.26 seconds (4:00.26) for 2026. The 2023–2026 comparison baseline is 233.62 seconds.

Pace index = baseline ÷ season adjusted time × 100

2025: 233.62 ÷ 230.58 × 100 = 101.3
2026: 233.62 ÷ 240.26 × 100 = 97.2

What does 2.8% slower mean in seconds?

The 2026 adjusted median is 240.26 seconds, while the four-year baseline is 233.62 seconds. That is 6.64 seconds slower than the baseline. Compared directly with 2025’s adjusted median of 230.58 seconds, 2026 is 9.68 seconds slower, or about 4.2% slower.

One caveat: 2025 contains six races, while 2026 currently contains five, so the 2026 result can still change when another qualifying race is added.

3. Calculate the season pace index

What does the difference mean in seconds?
